Artemis — Back to the Moon
What is Artemis?
NASA's return to the Moon — first crewed landing since 1972. Aims to land the first woman and first person of colour on the lunar surface. UK contributes the European Service Module that powers the Orion spacecraft.

AI & Space
Satellites generate more data than any human team can process — ML is the only way to keep up
UK Companies Using AI in Space
Satellites generate more data daily than any human team can process. ML now handles ocean plastic tracking, crop yield prediction, and debris removal.
Key Use Cases
🌍
Earth Observation
AI classifies land use, deforestation, flood extent and crop health from orbit — faster than any ground team.
🛰️
Autonomous Operations
On-board AI makes real-time decisions — when to fire thrusters, avoid debris, downlink critical images.
🗑️
Debris Removal
Computer vision guides robotic arms to capture defunct satellites — Astroscale UK ran the world's first commercial capture mission.
🌊
Ocean & Maritime
Spire's 110+ nanosatellites track 350,000+ ships — AI spots dark vessels, illegal fishing, and pollution.
🚀
Launch Optimisation
ML models optimise trajectory, fuel burn and staging — shaving weight and cost from every mission.
